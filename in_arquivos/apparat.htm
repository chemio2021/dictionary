Apparat (ap-uh-RAT, ah-puh-RAT) noun

 

   Structure, mechanism, etc. of an organization, especially a political one.

  

[From Russian apparat, from German, from Latin apparatus (equipment).]

 

  "Alice (Mahon) has no intention of handing her seat on to a miserable

   lackey of the hated apparat ..."

   Matthew Norman; Comment and Analysis: Diary; The Guardian (London, UK);

   Jul 27, 1999.

 

  "That seemed destined to change after the Second Vatican Council of 1962-65,

   which relaxed the grip of the papal apparat and elevated the importance of

   individual conscience."

   Bill Keller; Is the Pope Catholic?; The New York Times; May 4, 2002.
