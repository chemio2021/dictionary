Atomy (AT-uh-mee) noun
 
A skeleton.
 
[From the word anatomy taken as "an atomy". From Latin ana- (up) +
tome (a cutting). Ultimately from the Indo-European root tem- (to cut)
that is alto the source of tonsure, temple, epitome, and contemplate.]
 
-Anu Garg (words at wordsmith.org)
 
"That eyes, that are the frail'st and softest things,
Who shut their coward gates on atomies,
Should be call'd tyrants, butchers, murderers!"
William Shakespeare; As You Like It; 1599.
