Brainiac (BRAY-nee-ak) noun

 

   A very intelligent person.

 

adjective

 

   Highly intelligent.

 

[After Brainiac, a highly intelligent villainous character in the Superman comic strip.]

 

  "Who would you have bet on - the teenage coke-fiend or the brainiac Yalie?"

   John Patterson; Beautiful Minds; The Guardian (London, UK); Apr 25, 2003.

 

  "Don't be surprised if the person sipping a latte next to you is

   a brainiac from one of La Jolla's renowned research centers - the

   Scripps Institution of Oceanography or the Salk Institute for

   Biological Studies, founded by polio vaccine pioneer Jonas Salk."

   Katherine Kam; Good Looks, Money - and Brains, Too; San Francisco

   Examiner; Aug 20, 2000.
