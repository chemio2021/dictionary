Tangential (tan-JEN-shuhl) adjective

 

   1. Only slightly relevant to the matter in hand; digressive; divergent.

   2. Merely touching.

   3. Mathematics: Of or pertaining to the nature of a tangent.

 

[From Latin tangent-, tangens, present participle of tangere (to touch).]

 

The word tangential has numerous cousins, words derived from the same root:

tax, contact, attain, intact, tact, taste, tangible, tactile. What an

unlikely bunch of words to come out of the same parent! What's common in

all is the idea of touching (or not, as in case of "intact").

-Anu
