Vatic

PRONUNCIATION:
(VAT-ik)
MEANING:
adjective: Of or related to a prophet or a prophecy: prophetic.
 
ETYMOLOGY:
From Latin vates (prophet). Ultimately from the Indo-European root wet- (to blow or inspire) which is also the source of fan, atmosphere, Vatican, and Wednesday (literally, Woden's day, after a Norse god).
 
USAGE:
"'I know one day we will all die,' replied Adi, making a valiant stab at vatic foresight."
Tom Sutcliffe; Not All of It Added Up; The Independent (London, UK); Jan 29, 2009.
