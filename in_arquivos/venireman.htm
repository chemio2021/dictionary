Venireman (vi-NY-ree-muhn) noun
 
A person summoned as a prospective juror.
 
[From Latin venire (to come), truncation of the term venire facias
("you should cause to come", directing a sheriff to summon people
to serve as jurors) + man.]
 
Here are two related terms, often used interchangeably:
venire: the panel of prospective jurors
venire facias: the writ asking an official to summon a jury
 
-Anu Garg (words at wordsmith.org)
 
"Nearly every venireman wanted to join the jury, if for no other reason
than that it appeared to offer a front-row seat for the proceedings."
Edward J. Larson; Summer for the Gods: The Scopes Trial And America's
Continuing Debate over Science And Religion; Basic Books; 2006.
