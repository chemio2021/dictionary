Vox populi (VOKS POP-yuh-ly) noun

 

   Popular opinion; general sentiment.

 

[From Latin, literally voice of the people.]

 

A related expression is "Vox populi vox Dei" meaning the voice of

the people is the voice of God. It refers to the idea that the king

or the government ought to pay attention to the voice of the people.

Vox populi vox Dei certainly works when it comes to the growth of a

language.
