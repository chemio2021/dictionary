Wampum (WOM-puhm) noun

   1. Beads made from shells, strung in strands, belts, etc. used for
      ceremonial purposes, jewelry, and money.

   2. Money.

[Short for Massachusett wampompeag, from wampan (white) + api (string) +
-ag, plural suffix.]
