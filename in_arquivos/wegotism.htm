Wegotism

PRONUNCIATION:
(WEE-guh-tiz-uhm) 
 
MEANING:
noun: The habit of using “we” when referring to oneself.
 
ETYMOLOGY:
A blend of we + egotism. Earliest documented use: 1797. Also see nosism, royal we, and illeist.
 
USAGE:
“Dennis’s wegotism became ridiculous when he said, after someone stepped on his toes, ‘We don’t like people stepping on our toes.’”
Rod L. Evans; Thingamajigs and Whatchamacallits; Perigee; 2011.
